Lin Lab @ the University of Toronto

During the summer of 2023, I was funded by the ESROP - U of T fellowship and the CSB Undergraduate Research Award to work at the Lin Lab. The lab focuses on the study of systems neuroscience, specifically through implementing neuroimaging and computational tools to animal models to understand the neurodynamics behind cognition. My project aimed to create a virtual reality (VR) system for zebrafish, a popular model organism in neurobehavioural research. This unique opportunity combined biology, mechanical design, software, and optics, all of which are part of the many interdisciplinary fields I hoped to explore in my undergraduate years.

  • Developed an interactive, real-time VR system using C#, Python, and the Unity game engine. Learning Unity was a very exciting experience, and I found the program to be incredibly fun to use! I hope to explore more 3D modelling and game design software
  • Designed and built a VR platform compatible with neuroimaging microscopes using Arduinos, optomechanical components, and 3D printed parts
  • Designed and realized a logo for the lab! It combines many elements of the lab's expertise: a larval zebrafish representing the model organism, a microscope for neuroimaging, and a brain network to represent the study of neuroscience.

University of Toronto Department of Civil and Mineral Engineering

As my first research experience, I stepped foot into the fields of microbiology, building science, and public health, while also developing useful research skills and gaining exposure to a lab environment!

  • Developed and tested various particle collection methods for microbiological analyses
  • Utilized qPCR, RNA extraction, and DNA extraction to quantify SARS-CoV-2 virus concentrations in collected samples
  • Wrote both adapted and original standard operating procedures for experimental techniques
